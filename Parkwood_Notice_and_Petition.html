On or about April 27, 2026, the Board of Directors, through Association Manager Chris Bergeron of Prestige Association Management Group, purported to remove me immediately from the Board of Directors of Parkwood Place HOA — without evidence, without notice, and without any process permitted by our governing documents or Texas law.

The Governing Standard

What the Governing Documents Say

B-1 Bylaws, Article IV, Section 5 — Your HOA Handbook, Article IV

"No Member of the Board of Directors shall be removed from office except for malfeasance in the conduct of his or her duties."

This is the only permitted standard. "Malfeasance" requires specific, proven wrongdoing — vague references to "ethical concerns" or "best interests" do not qualify. The Board's removal letter contained none of the following:

  • Any specific act constituting malfeasance
  • Any factual findings, evidence, or written determination
  • Notice of any meeting at which removal was discussed or voted upon
  • An opportunity for Director Repka to respond or be heard
  • Minutes, voting records, or written consents showing a valid Board action

B-2 Declaration, Article XII, Section 3(a) — Your HOA Handbook, Article XII

This provision limits Board authority to act adversely against a director to circumstances involving willful misfeasance or bad faith. Stripping Director Repka of portal access without any such finding is itself a bad faith act outside the Board's lawful authority.

Violation by Violation

The Board's Own Conduct Constitutes Malfeasance — Citation by Citation

Each violation below cites the specific provision the Board broke. Click any statute link to read the full text at statutes.capitol.texas.gov.

B-1

Bylaws, Article IV, Section 5 — Removal Without Malfeasance

What it says: A director may only be removed for malfeasance in the conduct of duties.
What the Board did: Issued a removal letter citing this provision but identified zero specific acts of malfeasance. Referenced "ethical concerns" and "best interests" — language that does not satisfy the malfeasance standard.
✔ Verify: Your HOA Handbook — Article IV, Section 5
B-2

Declaration, Article XII, Section 3(a) — Bad Faith Action

What it says: Adverse action against a director requires a finding of willful misfeasance or bad faith.
What the Board did: Stripped Director Repka of portal access and Board communications without any such finding — conduct that is itself willful and in bad faith.
✔ Verify: Your HOA Handbook — Declaration, Article XII, Section 3(a)
T-1

Tex. Prop. Code § 209.005 — Unlawful Refusal to Produce Records

What it says: The Association must make books and records available for inspection and provide copies within 10 business days of a written request by a member.
What the Board did: Received a formal records request on May 6, 2026. Over 30 days later, not a single document has been produced. This violates Director Repka's rights and the rights of every homeowner to inspect Association records.
✔ Verify: statutes.capitol.texas.gov — search "Texas Property Code 209.005"
T-2

Tex. Prop. Code § 209.0051 — No Proper Notice of Board Meeting

What it says: Board meetings must be open, properly noticed at least 72 hours in advance, and Board actions must be taken at properly noticed, open meetings.
What the Board did: Took removal action and stripped portal access with no meeting notice provided, no open meeting held, and no agenda, minutes, or written consent ever produced.
✔ Verify: statutes.capitol.texas.gov — search "Texas Property Code 209.0051"
T-2B-1

§ 209.0051 + Bylaws — No Notice of Allegations or Opportunity to Be Heard

What it says: Tex. Prop. Code § 209.0051 requires proper notice before Board action. The Bylaws require compliance with governing procedures before removal. Together, these require that a director receive written notice of specific allegations and a meaningful opportunity to respond.
What the Board did: Issued an immediate removal letter with no prior notice of allegations, no hearing, and no opportunity to respond. Director Repka learned of the removal when the letter arrived.
✔ Verify: statutes.capitol.texas.gov — § 209.0051; HOA Handbook — Art. IV § 5
O-1

Tex. Bus. Orgs. Code § 22.102 — Board Must Act Within Governing Documents

What it says: A nonprofit corporation's board may only act within the authority granted by the entity's governing documents and applicable Texas law.
What the Board did: Acted entirely outside the authority granted by the Bylaws and Declaration — no malfeasance finding, no properly noticed meeting, no documented vote.
✔ Verify: statutes.capitol.texas.gov — search "Texas Business Organizations Code 22.102"
O-2

Tex. Bus. Orgs. Code § 22.211 — Director Removal Must Follow Governing Documents

What it says: Removal of a director of a nonprofit corporation must comply with the procedures set forth in the corporation's governing documents. The Bylaws are controlling.
What the Board did: Removed a director without following a single procedure required by the Bylaws — no malfeasance finding, no noticed meeting, no documented vote, no opportunity to be heard.
✔ Verify: statutes.capitol.texas.gov — search "Texas Business Organizations Code 22.211"
O-3

Tex. Bus. Orgs. Code § 20.002 — Ultra Vires Acts; Personal Exposure for Board Members

What it says: Acts taken by a governing authority that exceed its legal power under the governing documents constitute ultra vires acts that may be challenged and voided. Individual board members who authorized or participated in ultra vires acts may bear personal legal exposure.
What the Board did: Took removal action with no legal authority under the Bylaws, Declaration, or Texas law. Each Board member who voted for, authorized, or ratified this action participated in an ultra vires act.
✔ Verify: statutes.capitol.texas.gov — search "Texas Business Organizations Code 20.002"
